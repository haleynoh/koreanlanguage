세종대왕
Sejong the Great
Sejong of Joseon (The last dynastic kingdom of Korea) was the fourth ruler of the Joseon Kingdom. At the time, Koreans had their own verbal language but were using Chinese characters for writing. Many citizens were illiterate because Chinese characters were too difficult to learn and complicated for the Korean language. King Sejong's love for his citizens encouraged him to persevere and create sound-based characters that are easy to understand for his country despite the strong opposition. Thanks to King Sejong, Koreans have their own unique writing and culture today. He also appears on the 10,000 Won bill in Korean currency.
훈민정음
Hunminjeongeum
Hunminjeongeum (훈민정음) was the first Korean textbook published in 1446. It was created by King Sejong in 1443 with the help of scholars in Jiphyeonjeon. The language consists of 17 consonants and 11 vowels, with a total of 28 characters. Hunminjeongeum directly translates to “the right sounds to teach the people”, and it later evolves to become Hangeul (한글), which is the Korean alphabet that we know today.
